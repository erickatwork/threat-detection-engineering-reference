# Contributing

Thanks for helping improve the **Threat Detection Engineering Reference**! This is a curated list, so the bar for additions is *usefulness and quality*, not quantity.

## How to contribute

1. **Fork** the repo and create a branch for your change.
2. Make your edit in `README.md` (or the relevant file).
3. Open a **pull request** with a short description of what you added and why.

For small fixes (typos, broken links), you can edit directly on GitHub and open a PR from the browser.

## What makes a good addition

When adding a framework, tool, or resource, please:

- **Include a link** to the canonical source.
- **Add a one-line "why"** — what makes it worth a reader's time, not just what it is.
- **Place it under the most relevant heading.** If none fits, propose a new section in your PR description.
- **Keep formatting consistent** with surrounding entries (bullet style, link syntax).
- Prefer **primary, actively maintained sources** over blog reposts.

## What to avoid

- Low-effort link dumps with no context.
- Self-promotional links that don't add clear value to a detection engineer.
- Dead or paywalled resources where a free equivalent exists.

## Reporting issues

Spotted a broken link, outdated resource, or factual error? Open an
[issue](https://github.com/erickatwork/threat-detection-engineering-reference/issues)
— that's a valuable contribution too.

By contributing, you agree that your contributions will be licensed under the repository's [MIT License](LICENSE).
